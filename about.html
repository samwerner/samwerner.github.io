About

I am a PhD student in the Centre for Cryptocurrency Research and Engineering at Imperial College London, supervised by Professor William Knottenbelt. In my research I am trying to answer questions around designing stable blockchain systems. For instance, blockchains may be prone to instable transaction throughput due to employing a poorly designed difficulty adjustment algorithm or through a mining pool using a non-incentive compatible reward scheme. As part of my research I examine which types of instability risks exist and how these can be mitigated through robust blockchain design.

Previously I have worked as a software engineer intern at the enterprise blockchain firm R3 and have done contracting work for Imperial Consultants and Unilever.
